Green Energy

What is Green Energy?

  • Green energy is a type of energy that comes from natural sources that are constantly renewed, such as sunlight, wind, water, and heat from the Earth.
  • Unlike fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas, green energy sources do not pollute the air or harm the environment.
  • They produce little or no greenhouse gases, which helps fight climate change and protect the planet.
  • Common types of green energy include solar power, wind power, hydropower, geothermal energy, and biomass.
  • Using green energy is important because it provides a cleaner, safer, and more sustainable way to meet our energy needs today and in the future.
  • Wind turbines generate electricity using wind power.
  • Countries like Denmark get over 40% of their electricity from wind.
  • Wind farms are expanding worldwide to help move away from fossil fuels.

    • Hydropower

  • Hydropower uses the movement of water to generate electricity.
  • It captures energy from flowing rivers or falling water, usually through dams or turbines.
  • Moving water spins turbines connected to generators, producing clean and renewable electricity.
  • Hydropower is one of the oldest and most reliable sources of renewable energy.
  • It produces no air pollution or greenhouse gases, making it environmentally friendly.
  • Hydropower plants can provide a steady and consistent electricity supply.
  • This helps meet the energy needs of communities sustainably.
    • Solar energy is widely used in homes, schools, and communities because it is clean and renewable.
    • In Myanmar, many remote villages without access to the main power grid are installing small solar grids.
    • These solar projects provide light for studying at night and power schools.
    • Solar energy helps local businesses grow and improve the economy.
    • Using solar energy improves education and creates better living conditions.

    Why Green Energy Matters

    • Helps protect our planet and ensures a healthier future for everyone.
    • Produces little or no pollution, reducing harmful greenhouse gases that cause climate change.
    • Conserves natural resources by using renewable sources like the sun, wind, and water.
    • Improves air quality and reduces health problems caused by pollution.
    • Supports sustainable economic growth.
    • Provides clean, safe, and reliable power while protecting the environment for future generations.
